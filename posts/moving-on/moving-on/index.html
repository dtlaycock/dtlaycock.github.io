Moving on

   2019-01-24  

Thirteen years is a really long time to work anywhere. I used to joke that it was like a prison sentence, except that is unfair even during the darker times.

Looking back on my experiences over the last 13 years there is a lot of good, and a lot to be thankful for. However I realized that most of the people that I would thank were no longer at the company. Hence this (belated) post.

I joined The Economist in 2005 working within the EIU as a developer for an in house template / publishing system for the reports that the EIU produces. Technology wise it was familiar ground for me, so after a couple years it was time for a change.

I joined the Economist.com team as an inexperienced web developer. The site at the time was a custom built CMS, with ColdFusion generating the site. The learning curve was steep and the

Stew Robinson was a big presence on the team for many years. So it is particularly strange that the name has faded from the organizational memory. It is Stew that gave me the opportunity to join the team despite not having a lick of ColdFusion experience. He also had a hand in the decision to migrate the site to Drupal. Up until recently I would highlight those as the biggest breaks during my career. Drupal has been the foundational technology of my time at The Economist. Moving from a custom CMS and ColdFusion to Drupal was a complete transformation for the engineering team and being part of the Drupal community has been

If Drupal was the core of my career at The Economist from a technology perspective, agile and scrum were revolutionary
